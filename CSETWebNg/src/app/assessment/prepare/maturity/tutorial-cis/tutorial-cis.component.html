Cyber Infrastructure Survey (CIS) Tutorial

Overview

The goal of the Cyber Infrastructure Survey (CIS) is to assess the foundational and essential cybersecurity practices of an organization's critical service and evaluate the effectiveness of security controls, cybersecurity preparedness, and the resilience of an organization's cybersecurity posture. The CIS focuses on a single critical service rather than all organizational operations.

Scope
Determine the survey scope by considering the following factors:
  • Critical Service: Which service will be the focus of the survey?
  • Business Unit/Agency: Which part(s) of the organization deliver the critical service?
  • Asset(s): Which assets (people, technology, information, and facilities) are required for delivery of the service?
Service Types
A basic principle to remember throughout the survey is the difference between a critical service and a cyber service.
  • Critical Service (CS): A set of important activities that the organization carries out, in the production of a product or while providing services to its customers. A service that the loss thereof would result in physical destruction, safety, and health effects (e.g., a chemical release or loss of traffic controls), theft of sensitive information that can be exploited, business interruption (e.g., denial of service), or other economic loss to the organization or its customers/users.
  • Cyber Service: Any combination of equipment and devices (hardware); applications and platforms (software), communications, and data that is integrated to provide specific cyber services.
Asset Types
The CIS considers four categories of assets: People, Information, Technology, and Facilities. The definition for each asset type is listed below:
  • People: the staff (both internal and external to the organization) such as people that support data centers or otherwise use information and communications technology to operate, monitor, and support the service.
  • Information: account information, technology asset configuration files, operational data, customer information, and other information necessary for the delivery of a service.
  • Technology: computers (hardware), software, control systems, or other technology including external information systems used by the organization to deliver the service.
  • Facilities: office buildings, data centers, and other physical structures supporting the delivery of the service.
Assessment
The CIS assessment consists of 71 questions across 28 sub-domains. Each sub-domain aligns with one of the five following domains:
  • Cybersecurity Management
  • Cybersecurity Forces
  • Cybersecurity Controls
  • Incident Response
  • Dependencies

Each practice includes the following components.

Sub-Domain Score

Each sub-domain page includes a chart that displays a score based on how the associated questions are answered. If a baseline survey was selected on the Critical Service Information page, the corresponding baseline score will also be displayed.

Sub-Domain

Each sub-domain has one or more associated questions. A brief overview of its purpose and suggested approaches are provided alongside each sub-domain.

Practice Identifier

Each question has an assigned practice identifier.

Question

Each question is designed to evaluate if the organization has implemented the associated sub-domains.

Information Icon

Select the information icon to view the CSET icons.

Answer Buttons

The survey includes a variety of question types, including single-choice, multiple choice, and free text. Select the applicable answer(s) for each question. Some questions may be multi-level and certain sections may be enabled or removed based on the selected answer.

Inconsistency Warning

An inconsistency warning appears when the selected answer contradicts an answer given for a previous question. The warning includes the contradicted question(s).
